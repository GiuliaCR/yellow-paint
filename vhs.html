Queer temporality is folded time. Encountering our own queer history through our memory folding back onto itself, revealing more of our true self with each fold.

Rewinding through my childhood videos I try to spot signs of what’s to come, revisiting them as a portal to my own history, illuminated by my present knowledge. Did my younger self already know who she would become? Where she would go? Who she would love?

There is a freedom in the way I move my body, unencumbered by any desire to adhere to societal expectation or simply unaware. In a way, this younger self seems closer to my present self that then closeted years in between – they are me when I am free to be my full self.

Folding time is the continuous/growing/looping/never-ending process of self-understanding and becoming.
